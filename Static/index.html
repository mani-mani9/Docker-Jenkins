About Ongole Bulls

The Ongole bull, also known as the Ongole cattle or Ongole breed, originates from the Ongole region in Prakasam district, Andhra Pradesh, India. These bulls are globally recognized for their strength, endurance, and disease resistance. The breed is known for its muscular build and ability to thrive in harsh climatic conditions.

Characteristics

  • Large, strong, and well-built cattle breed
  • High resistance to diseases
  • Capable of surviving in extreme climatic environments
  • Known for their distinctive hump and strong horns
  • Widely used in breeding programs around the world

History & Recognition

The Ongole cattle breed has been exported to several countries including the United States, Brazil, Mexico, and Australia. Many famous global cattle strains like the Brahman breed have genetic roots linked back to the Ongole breed.

Uses

  • Draught power for agricultural activities
  • Breeding programs worldwide
  • High-quality milk production (from cows)
  • Cultural importance in events and festivals
